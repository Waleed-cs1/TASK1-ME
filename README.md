1. Body Shape and Frame: A solid, rectangular frame intended to keep the center of gravity low and centralize component weight.
2. Leg Design: To facilitate effective mechanical movement, articulated L-shaped legs with two segments (thigh and shank)
3. Number of Joints and DOF: Each leg has two joints and two degrees of freedom. Since the robot has four legs, the total number of joints is 8, and the total number of Degrees of Freedom (DOF) is 8.

4. Motor Type: Motor with gearbox and higher torque

5. Preliminary Torque Calculation: Assuming the robot weighs 3 kg, each leg supports approximately 0.75 kg while standing. The applied force is about 7.36 N. With a distance of 0.08 m between the joint and the foot, the required torque is approximately 0.59 N·m, which is within the capability of the selected motor.

6. Stability and Center of Gravity: To achieve stability, the center of gravity should be close to the center of the body

7. Proposed Walking Method: Proposed Walking Method: Linear Motion, where the robot moves in a straight path.

8. Expected Mechanical Problems: Expected Mechanical Problems:
A- Poor robot balance.
B- Leg slipping.
C- Fast battery drain.
